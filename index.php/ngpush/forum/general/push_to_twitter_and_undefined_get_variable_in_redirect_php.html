Hi Vincent,

you are right, the link should have the oauth_token GET parameter populated.

Token requesting happens in ngPushTwitterBase::requestToken method.

Couple of things come to mind why it wouldn't work.

1) Wrong consumer key & secret (please doublecheck for any typos, whitespaces or such)

2) Did you regenerate autoload array after installing Netgen Push?

I can't provide any more help without some more debugging on your part, unfortunately. But now you know where the access token gets generated so you can do so :)
